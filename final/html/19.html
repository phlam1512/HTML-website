Asians are Smarter and are Better at Mathematics

This is a positive stereotype towards Asians, and in particular Asian-Americans. Many teacher often ponders the stereotype that Asians are better at maths, and the despair that everybody else from white on does are disadvantaged. The common believe, or stereotype being is that Asian students are inherently food at maths, and better than anyone else, and such stereotyping of Asian students goes on unabated, throughout the society. The general attitude towards such stereotyping is that Asians are good at math because they’re smarter, can learn maths much faster, knows “stuff” more deeply, and are much quicker at arriving at answers. And nonetheless, Asian students are good at math because, after all, they’re Asian.

Such stereotyping also extends and continues in the ‘adult world’. “It gives those of us who are not of Asian extraction the excuse not to perform at a high level in mathematics, science, engineering, or other similar pursuits. After all, Asians are automatically better, so why try?”

A biracial participant says:
“Asians are good at math. What an awful stereotype. I met a guy [whose] family had moved from a large city on west coast to a small town in Oklahoma and he was Asian and absolutely awful in math—could not do it, and it was [in] good humour. That’s the stereotype: Asians are good at math. And his friends started poking fun at him, and it was something to laugh at.”

The main reason being is that is it the Asians who are doing well in Maths, Sciences and Engineering who chose to come here (for instance, America) at graduate degree level or higher, and they stay here as Physicians, Theoretical Physics, Mathematicians, Engineers, Chemists, Computer Science, Microbiologists, just to name a few. And since those schools are highly populated with such Asian students, it inevitable produces the illusion of Asians are smarter and better at maths.

In addition, similarity to the previously discussed stereotype on “White People are Racist”, this is nonetheless a simple logical fallacy.
