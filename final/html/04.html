Stereotypes

Common definitions of stereotypes by surveyed students

Definition of stereotypes by different online dictionaries and Wikipedia
  • ◼ A belief or thought that does not reflect the reality: 50%
  • ◼ An idea that people have about others that is wrong: 35%
  • ◼ A belief about a group of people that is negative: 15%
  • ◼ A thought about a group of people doing something in a certain way: 5%
Cambridge Dictionaries Online A set idea that people have about what someone or something is like, especially an idea that is wrong, such as:
  • racial or sexual stereotypes
  • "He doesn't confrom to/fit/fill the national stereotype of a Frechman"
  • "The characters in the book are just stereotypes"
Dictionary.com Sociology. A simplified and standardised conception or image invested with special meaning and held in common by members of a group. For example:
  • "The cowboy and Indian are American stereotypes."
Merriam-Webster To believe unfairly that all people or things with a particular characteristic are the same.
Oxford Dictionaries A widely held but fixed and oversimplified image or idea of a particular type of person or thing, such as:
  • sexual and racial stereotypes
  • the stereotype of the woman as the carer
Wikipedia In social psychology, a stereotype is a thought that can be adopted about specific types of individuals or certain ways of doing things. These thoughts or beliefs may or may not accurately reflect reality.
