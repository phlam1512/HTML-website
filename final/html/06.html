Linking Stereotypes and Discrimination

Stereotypes

A simplified concept, image or idea that categorises a group of individuals or people that are similar. However, these thoughts or beliefs may or may not accurately reflect the reality nature of things.


Review

Discrimination

The act or treatment to the categorised people (otherwise known as stereotypes), usually in an unfair and worse way than the way people are usually treated.


Review

Analysis

Using the definitions researched in the previous two inquiry questions, as well as a number of additional sources, although nowadays people often use the terms “stereotype” and “discrimination” interchangeably, there is a definite difference between these two terms.

Stereotype is similar to Prejudice, where a Stereotype is the belief about a particular group of people; Prejudice is the feeling or belief about an individual or a group of people based on their membership in the group. Both of these terms categorises an individual or a group of people based on a biased thought or belief, no matter positive or negative. On the other hand, as Stereotype is the categorisation of particulars of the society based on biased thoughts or beliefs, discrimination is the act or treatment to these particulars, and often these particulars are being treated in an unfair and worse way than the other members of the society.

In conclusion, with the biased thoughts and beliefs of particular individuals or a group of people pre-existing among the society, also known as Stereotypes, these biased thoughts and beliefs of these particulars have caused them to be treated unfairly by the rest of the society, also known as Discrimination. In other words, Stereotypes are the biased thoughts and beliefs, and Discrimination is the unfair acts based on these thoughts and beliefs.
