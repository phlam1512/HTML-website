Discrimination

Common definitions of discrimination by surveyed students

Definition of discrimination by different online dictionaries and Wikipedia
  • ◼ The act of treating a group of people badly as compared to others: 40%
  • ◼ The unequal treatment of a specific group of people based on their background: 35%
  • ◼ The treatment in against to a specific group of people based on their background: 15%
  • ◼ The treatment of people being treated in a way that is worse than others: 15%
Cambridge Dictionaries Online [The] ​treating a ​person or ​particular ​group of ​people ​differently, ​especially in a ​worse way from the way in which you ​treat other ​people, because of ​their ​skin ​colour, ​sex, ​sexuality, etc., for example:
  • racial/​sex/​age discrimination
  • ‘Until 1986 most ​companies would not ​even ​allow women to take the ​exams, but such ​blatant discrimination is now ​disappearing.’
Dictionary.com [The] treatment or consideration of, or making a distinction in favour of or against, a person or thing based on the group, class, or category to which that person or thing belongs rather than on individual merit. For example:
  • ‘racial and religious intolerance and discrimination.’
Merriam-Webster The practice of unfairly treating a person or group of people differently from other people or groups of people.
Oxford Dictionaries The unjust or prejudicial treatment of different categories of people, especially on the grounds of race, age, or sex, such as:
  • ’victims of racial discrimination’
  • ‘discrimination against homosexuals’
Wikipedia Discrimination is treatment or consideration of, or making a distinction in favour of or against, a person or thing based on the group, class, or category to which that person or thing is perceived to belong to rather than on individual merit. This includes treatment of an individual or group based on their actual or perceived membership in a certain group or social category, "in a way that is worse than the way people are usually treated".
